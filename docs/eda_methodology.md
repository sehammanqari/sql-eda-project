# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Methodology

## Overview

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is the process of examining and understanding a dataset to uncover patterns, detect anomalies, and generate insights. The main objective of EDA is to gain familiarity with the data before performing deeper analytical or modeling tasks.

In this project, the EDA process follows a structured methodology consisting of six main steps:

1. Database Exploration
2. Dimension Exploration
3. Date Exploration
4. Measures Exploration
5. Magnitude Analysis
6. Ranking Analysis

Each step focuses on a specific aspect of the dataset to progressively build a deeper understanding of the data.

---

# 1. Database Exploration

## Definition

Database exploration focuses on understanding the **structure of the dataset**.

This step involves identifying:

* Available tables
* Column names
* Data types
* Number of records
* Relationships between tables

## Purpose

The goal is to understand **how the data is organized** and determine which tables and fields are relevant for analysis.

---

# 2. Dimension Exploration

## Definition

Dimension exploration focuses on analyzing **categorical attributes** within the dataset.

Examples of dimensions include:

* Product categories
* Customer countries
* Gender
* Customer segments

## Purpose

The goal is to understand:

* The distinct values within each dimension
* The distribution of categories
* How data is grouped across these attributes

This helps identify important segments in the data.

---

# 3. Date Exploration

## Definition

Date exploration analyzes **time-related attributes** to identify patterns and trends over time.

Examples of date attributes include:

* Order date
* Shipping date
* Transaction date

## Purpose

This step helps analysts discover:

* Seasonal patterns
* Growth trends
* Changes in business activity over time

Time analysis is critical for understanding **business performance and trends**.

---

# 4. Measures Exploration

## Definition

Measures exploration focuses on **numerical metrics** within the dataset.

Examples of measures include:

* Sales
* Revenue
* Quantity
* Profit

## Purpose

This step helps analysts understand the **general behavior of numerical data** by calculating:

* Total values
* Average values
* Minimum values
* Maximum values

These metrics provide a quick overview of the scale and distribution of key business measures.

---

# 5. Magnitude Analysis

## Definition

Magnitude analysis compares the **size or contribution of different groups** within the data.

Examples include:

* Sales by product category
* Revenue by country
* Orders by customer segment

## Purpose

The goal is to identify **which groups contribute the most or least** to overall performance.

This helps highlight the most important drivers of business results.

---

# 6. Ranking Analysis

## Definition

Ranking analysis identifies the **top-performing and lowest-performing entities** within the dataset.

Examples include:

* Top customers by revenue
* Best-selling products
* Lowest-performing categories

## Purpose

This step helps analysts quickly identify **leaders and underperformers** using techniques such as:

* Top N queries
* Bottom N queries
* Ranking functions

Ranking analysis is commonly used to support **business decision-making and performance evaluation**.

---

# Summary

The EDA methodology used in this project provides a systematic framework for exploring and understanding data. By progressing through database structure, dimensions, time patterns, numerical measures, magnitude comparisons, and ranking analysis, analysts can gain meaningful insights and build a strong foundation for further analytical work.
